As of Sept 23rd 2016, SquirrelMail is no longer offered as a Webmail option. atmail is the new and improved Webmail application available to all DreamHost users.

Accessing atmail webmail using the DreamHost URL

Visit the following URL to log into your atmail webmail service:

http://atmail.dreamhost.com

or

https://atmail.dreamhost.com
The atmail login page opens:

Webmail is automatically configured to connect to the DreamHost mail server. The only credentials you must enter are your email address and password.

Security warning when using HTTPS with your website URL

You can also log into atmail at https://atmail.example.com (where example.com is your website). However, this throws a certificate warning.

This is because the security certificate used by Webmail is written for the dreamhost.com domain, not your personal domain. When you see the warning, accept this certificate and save it in your browser. You should only need to do this once to avoid future warning messages. View the Certificate Domain Mismatch Error article for further details.

Checking for new mail

atmail, by default, does not automatically check for new mail if you leave it open.

  • To check for new mail manually, click the refresh button near the top left.

Changing your timezone

Webmail defaults to the server's timezone. You can change it to your local time if you like by following these steps:

  1. Log into Webmail at http://atmail.dreamhost.com.
  2. Click the Settings tab at the top.
  3. Scroll toward the bottom, and then click the dropdown titled 'Timezone'.
  4. Select your desired timezone.
  5. Click the Save Settings button at the bottom of the page.

File attachments

Webmail is a PHP application and the max attachment size available is 40 MB.

The Spam folder

The Spam/Junk folder used with the panel's Anti-spam filter is not available in atmail. If you enable Anti-spam filters in the panel, you must enter a folder in the field 'Quarantine to IMAP folder'. After you enter a folder name, your Spam emails are filtered into this new folder of your choosing.

The current Spam folder you see is not functional regarding automated filtering. In the near future, DreamHost will implement an automated filter used with atmail.

Filtering emails into the Spam folder

Contacts

Adding a contact

To add a new contact:

  1. Click the 'Add Contact' button on the top left.
  2. In the right pane, enter the contact details.
  3. When finished, click the Save Contact button at the bottom.

Deleting a contact

  1. Highlight the contact.
  2. Make sure the checkbox is 'checked'.
  3. Click the trashcan icon to delete it.

There is no confirmation message to confirm the deletion. If you click the Trash icon, the contact is immediately deleted.

Adding a contact image

To add an image to the contact, simply click the silhouette icon:

Search your computer for an image, and then save it.

Adding a group

  1. Click the 'Add Group' icon on the top left.
  2. Enter your group name.
  3. If necessary, drag and drop contacts into this group.

Exporting contacts

  1. Select the contacts you wish to export.
  2. Click the Export icon on the top left.
  3. Choose to save as a .vcf or .csv file.

Importing contacts

  1. Click the import icon on the top left.
  2. Select the file to import on your home computer.
    The contacts are immediately imported.

Migrating contacts from Roundcube or SquirrelMail

Any contacts you had in Roundcube and/or SquirrelMail are automatically migrated into atmail when you first sign in. There is currently no way to disable this feature.

If you do NOT wish to migrate your contacts, please remove them from Roundcube and/or SquirrelMail prior to switching your domain to atmail. No contacts are automatically removed during this process, and if you ever decide to revert back to Roundcube and/or SquirrelMail all your contact information will still be present.

First Name, Last Name, and Email Address are the only fields that can migrate at this time. Since atmail does not have a facility for providing a contact a "Nickname", this information is lost from SquirrelMail.
