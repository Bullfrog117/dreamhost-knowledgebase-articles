Comcast has set up rate limiting on all inbound email traffic. As a result, it's possible you may see bounced messages when sending to a Comcast email address.

Below is an example of what you may see:

<USERNAME@COMCAST.NET>: delivery temporarily suspended: host
mx2.comcast.net[68.87.20.5] refused to talk to me: 421
resimta-ch2-18v.sys.comcast.net comcast 66.33.216.122 Throttled -
try again later. GEmL1te232g1bk301 
Please see
http://postmaster.comcast.net/smtp-error-codes.php#RL000002

If you click on the link at the bottom, it takes you to a page with further details. For example:

"Comcast has instituted rate limiting on all inbound email traffic. This
step was taken to protect our customers and our email system from
unwanted spam and malicious attacks. Any systems affected by this rate
limit will receive a 4xx message (temp-fail) during the SMTP transaction.
This message is designed to instruct the sending server to try again at a
later time to deliver its email."

What can I do about this error?

This is not a Mailblock issue. This is an issue with with greylisting. So one thing you can do to help prevent this is to configure SPF records for your domain. View the following articles for details:

  • SPF overview
  • What SPF records do I use?

If your email is hosted with DreamHost, you should add the SPF record listed under the 'DreamHost SPF records' section.

  • How do I add an SPF record?

If you find your email is continuing to fail after setting up SPF records, contact DreamHost support with the details of your email failure. Make sure to provide all information listed in the following article:
